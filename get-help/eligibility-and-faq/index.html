Eligibility & Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about staying at Brother's Place in Rome, GA — for men in need and their families.

Who We Serve

Brother's Place serves adult men experiencing homelessness, addiction, and/or mental-health challenges who are ready to engage in a structured, Christ-centered recovery environment. We welcome men of all faith backgrounds.

Basic Eligibility

  • Adult male (18+)
  • Willing to participate in program activities and house expectations
  • Committed to sobriety (completion of detox/rehab preferred but not required)
  • Open to living in a Christ-centered community
  • Able to complete an intake process (application + screening call)

Frequently Asked Questions

We work with each resident individually to remove financial barriers. Our goal is never to turn a man away because of money. Contact us at 706.509.9020 to discuss your situation.

The typical length of stay is 6 to 12 months, depending on individual needs and progress. We create a personalized plan with each resident — we don't rush the process because lasting recovery takes time.

We believe in grace and accountability. A single relapse does not automatically mean dismissal. We evaluate each situation individually, considering honesty, willingness to recommit, and the safety of our community. Our goal is restoration, not punishment.

Yes. We encourage healthy family relationships. Visitation policies and schedules are shared during intake. We also provide guidance for families navigating the recovery process alongside their loved one.

Bring personal identification (state ID or driver's license), any medications with prescriptions, about a week's worth of clothing, and basic personal hygiene items. We provide bedding, towels, meals, and most other necessities. If you don't have these items, that's okay — come as you are.

Brother's Place is a Christ-centered supportive housing and second step recovery program. While similar to a halfway house in providing structured transitional housing, we offer more comprehensive programming including recovery classes, spiritual formation, case management, and job training — all rooted in faith.

You can apply online through our application page or call 706.509.9020. Applications are reviewed within 1–2 business days, followed by a screening conversation.

We evaluate each applicant individually. A criminal background does not automatically disqualify anyone. We assess readiness for recovery, willingness to participate in the program, and safety considerations for the community.
