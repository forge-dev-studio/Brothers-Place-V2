Local Recovery & Crisis Resources in Rome, GA

If Brother's Place isn't the right fit, these organizations in Rome, Floyd County, and Northwest Georgia can help.

We believe in community — not competition. If our program isn't the right fit for you or your loved one, we want to make sure you find help. Here are trusted resources in the Rome, GA area:

🚨 Crisis & Emergency

National Suicide Prevention Lifeline

Call or text 988 — available 24/7

SAMHSA National Helpline

Free, confidential, 24/7 treatment referral and information: 1-800-662-4357

Highland Rivers Behavioral Health

Local behavioral health and crisis services for Floyd County. Crisis line: 1-800-715-3577

🏠 Shelters & Housing

William S. Davies Shelter

Emergency shelter in Rome, GA, for men, women, and families experiencing homelessness.

Salvation Army of Rome

Emergency assistance, food pantry, and transitional support services in Floyd County.

🤝 Recovery & Support Groups

Alcoholics Anonymous (AA) — Rome, GA

Multiple weekly meetings in the Rome area. Find meetings at aa.org/find-aa

Narcotics Anonymous (NA) — Rome, GA

Recovery support meetings in Floyd County. Find meetings at na.org

Celebrate Recovery

Christ-centered 12-step recovery program. Multiple churches in the Rome area host weekly meetings.

🧠 Mental Health Services

Floyd County Community Service Board

Outpatient mental health and substance abuse services for Floyd County residents.

Georgia Crisis & Access Line

Statewide behavioral health crisis line: 1-800-715-3577

Still Not Sure Where to Turn?

Call us at 706.509.9020. Even if Brother's Place isn't the right fit, we'll do our best to connect you with someone who can help.
