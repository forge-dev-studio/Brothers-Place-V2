Our Story

How faith, lived experience, and a love for Rome, Georgia, birthed a ministry of hope for men in crisis.

Founders

Meet TK & Claudia Hamilton

📷 Photo of TK & Claudia Hamilton — placeholder for real photography

TK and Claudia Hamilton are both natives of Rome, Georgia, with deep, lifelong ties to the recovery community in Floyd County. They have faced numerous challenges in their own lives — but through the love of Jesus Christ, they overcame and chose to share that same hope with others.

Together, they founded Brother's Place, a faith-based organization that offers safe and stable housing, support groups, and community case management to men struggling with addiction, homelessness, and mental-health challenges — and to the families walking alongside them.

TK and Claudia recognized something that many in their community saw but few addressed: the underlying issues that contribute to homelessness — trauma, addiction, mental illness, and spiritual brokenness — require more than temporary fixes. They require the transformative love of Christ and a community committed to walking alongside men through every stage of recovery.

"We've been where these men are. We know what it feels like to be broken, to feel like there's no way out. But through Christ, we found our way — and now we want to help others find theirs."

— TK & Claudia Hamilton, Founders

How It Began

Brother's Place was born out of a simple but powerful conviction: every man deserves a chance to rebuild his life with dignity, support, and the love of Christ.

In the neighborhoods of Rome, GA, TK and Claudia saw men cycling through homelessness, addiction, and incarceration with nowhere to turn. The existing system offered crisis intervention, but little in the way of long-term, faith-centered supportive housing that addressed the whole person — body, mind, and spirit.

They opened the doors of the Maple Street home in 2023, starting with a handful of residents and an abundance of faith. What began as a small act of obedience has grown into a ministry that provides housing, case management, spiritual formation, job training, and a genuine community of brothers walking the road to recovery together.

Our Mission

Empowering, equipping and enriching men through the LOVE of Christ.

Everything we do flows from this mission. From the meals we share in our kitchen to the Bible studies around our dining table, from the case-management meetings to the job-training sessions — Christ's love is the source and the sustaining power.

Our Guiding Scripture

"You led them with a pillar of cloud by day, and with a pillar of fire by night, to illuminate the way they should go."

— Nehemiah 9:12

Why Christ-Centered?

We believe that lasting recovery — the kind that transforms a man's life from the inside out — begins with a relationship with Jesus Christ. Not religion as a rulebook, but faith as a lifeline.

At Brother's Place, Christ is named openly as the source of transformation. But our doors are open to any man willing to engage, regardless of where he is on his faith journey. We don't require belief as a condition of entry — we offer it as a path to healing, with love, patience, and without coercion.

Our experience, both personal and ministerial, has shown us again and again: when men encounter genuine love — the kind that names Christ as its origin — everything changes. That's why we do what we do.
