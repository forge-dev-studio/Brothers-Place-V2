Second Step Program in Rome, GA.

Structured, Christ-centered aftercare for men ready for the next phase of recovery.

What Is a Second Step Program?

A second step program is structured transitional housing for men who have completed detox or inpatient rehab. It bridges the gap between intensive treatment and independent living. Residents live in a safe, substance-free home while attending recovery classes, working with a case manager, and rebuilding the daily routines that support long-term sobriety.

Unlike standard sober living, a second step program provides structured programming. Mandatory classes, spiritual formation, job training, and accountability are all designed to address the root causes of addiction and prevent relapse.

At Brother's Place, the typical program length is 6 to 12 months, individualized to each man's needs and progress. We do not rush the process. Lasting recovery takes time.

How the Program Works

Three pillars hold the Second Step Program together. Every man works on all three at the same time.

Substance-Free Housing

A clean, structured home environment with shared meals, daily routines, and the brotherhood of men working the same recovery road.

Recovery and Faith Classes

Weekly relapse prevention, faith-based recovery teaching, Bible study, and pastoral mentoring woven through the rhythm of every week.

Job Training and Case Management

Resume help, interview prep, financial literacy, and one-on-one case management connecting each man to benefits, healthcare, and employers.

Classes Included in the Program

The Second Step Program includes structured recovery, life-skills, and faith-based classes taught on a weekly schedule.

Relapse Prevention

Evidence-informed strategies for identifying triggers, building coping skills, and maintaining sobriety long term.

Faith-Based Recovery

Biblical principles applied to the recovery journey. Exploring how faith in Christ provides the ultimate foundation for healing.

Job Readiness and Resume

Resume building, interview preparation, workplace skills, and connections with local employers across Rome and Floyd County.

Financial Literacy

Budgeting, saving, credit repair, and financial planning. The skills for long-term independence after the program ends.

A Typical Journey Through the Program

Each man's plan is personalized, but most journeys follow a similar arc across 6 to 12 months.

Admission

Application, intake interview, and welcome into the house. Men who have completed inpatient rehab, detox, or who are actively in recovery are eligible. Apply through our online application or call 706.509.9020.

Month 1, Stabilization

Settle into the daily rhythm. Begin classes, devotion, case management, and house responsibilities. Set initial goals.

Months 2 to 4, Building Foundations

Recovery classes deepen. Mentoring relationships form. Job-readiness work begins. First employment or GED steps.

Months 5 to 8, Working the Plan

Steady employment, savings, and continued faith formation. Quarterly reviews track progress and adjust the plan.

Months 9 to 12, Step-Down Planning

Permanent housing search, ongoing aftercare connections, alumni community integration, and graduation when ready.
