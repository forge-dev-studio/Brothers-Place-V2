Supportive Housing for Men in Rome, GA

A safe, stable home at 1709 Maple Street — where recovery begins with dignity.

📷 Photo of the Maple Street home exterior — placeholder for real photography

A Place to Call Home

At 1709 Maple Street in Rome, Georgia, Brother's Place offers men a clean, dignified living environment as the foundation for their recovery journey. Our supportive housing isn't just about a roof and a bed — it's about creating a community where men can heal, grow, and prepare for independent living.

Every resident receives a private or shared room in our fully furnished home, three meals a day, access to laundry facilities, and the support of housemates and staff who understand what they're going through — because many of us have been there ourselves.

What Residents Receive

🛏️ Safe, Clean Housing

Furnished rooms in a well-maintained home with shared common areas, kitchen, and living space.

🍽️ Three Daily Meals

Nutritious meals prepared in-house. Shared mealtimes are part of building community.

👥 Peer Community

Live alongside other men who are committed to recovery. Accountability and brotherhood.

📋 Structured Routine

Daily schedules with devotion, house duties, programming, and evening check-ins.

A Day in the Life

Here's what a typical day looks like at Brother's Place:

6:30 AM — Morning Devotion

Start the day with scripture, prayer, and reflection.

7:00 AM — Breakfast

Shared meal around the table with your brothers.

8:00 AM — House Responsibilities

Cleaning, laundry, meal prep — shared duties build pride and structure.

9:00 AM — Programming

Recovery classes, case management meetings, job-readiness sessions.

12:00 PM — Lunch

Midday meal and fellowship.

1:00 PM — Work / Education

Employment, job search, GED classes, or approved activities.

5:30 PM — Dinner

Evening meal together.

7:00 PM — Bible Study / Group Session

Faith-based study, support group, or guest speaker.

9:00 PM — Evening Check-in

Reflect on the day, set intentions for tomorrow.
